---
title: "Tracking your physical cash inventory"
description: "Understand how TransactApp's cash inventory feature lets you record and monitor the physical cash you have on hand, separate from your transaction ledger."
---

Cash inventory in TransactApp refers to the physical money you have available — the bills and coins in your wallet or at home. Unlike the transaction ledger, which records individual income and expense events, the cash inventory gives you a snapshot of how much physical cash you currently hold. Keeping both in sync gives you a complete and accurate view of your personal finances.

<Note>
  The cash inventory balance and the transaction ledger balance are two separate figures. The transaction ledger tracks all income and expense events over time; the cash inventory tracks the actual physical cash you have on hand right now. They will only match if all your transactions involve cash — if you also use bank transfers, cards, or digital payments, the two values will naturally differ.
</Note>

## What cash inventory tracks

When you record your cash inventory, you are telling TransactApp how much physical money you currently hold. This is useful if you:

- Spend and receive cash regularly and want to know your exact cash on hand at any time.
- Budget with physical cash (for example, using the envelope method).
- Want to compare your digital transaction history against your actual cash position.

## Recording your cash on hand

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the cash inventory section">
    Open TransactApp and go to the cash inventory view from the main navigation. This is a separate section from the transactions list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your current cash amount">
    Input the total amount of physical cash you currently have. You can enter the full sum directly (for example, 250.00) rather than breaking it down by denomination, unless your version of the app supports denomination-level entry.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your cash balance">
    Confirm the entry. TransactApp records your current cash inventory and stores it locally. The cash inventory balance is now updated to reflect what you entered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Updating your cash balance

Your cash balance changes whenever you spend or receive physical cash. To keep it accurate, update the inventory after cash transactions:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="After spending cash">
    When you pay for something with physical money, record the expense in your transaction ledger and then update your cash inventory to reflect the reduced amount. Subtract what you spent from your current cash balance and enter the new total.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="After receiving cash">
    When you receive physical money — a cash payment, withdrawal from an ATM, or any other source — record the income in your transaction ledger and then increase your cash inventory balance accordingly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Periodic reconciliation">
    Count your physical cash periodically and compare it to the cash inventory balance in TransactApp. If the numbers differ, update the balance to match your actual cash on hand. Small discrepancies are normal over time, and correcting them keeps your records accurate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Cash inventory vs. transaction ledger

| | Cash inventory | Transaction ledger |
|---|---|---|
| **What it tracks** | Physical cash on hand right now | All income and expense events over time |
| **How it is updated** | Manually, whenever your cash changes | One entry per transaction |
| **Running balance** | Single current balance | Cumulative balance of all entries |
| **Best used for** | Knowing how much cash you have available | Reviewing your full financial history |

## Why tracking both matters

Recording transactions alone does not tell you how much physical cash you have available at any given moment — especially if some of your income and expenses involve non-cash payment methods. By maintaining both the transaction ledger and the cash inventory:

- You can verify that your cash on hand matches what your records predict.
- You get a complete picture of your finances: both the history of events and your current cash position.
- Discrepancies between the two can surface errors, forgotten transactions, or spending you have not yet recorded.

<Tip>
  Make updating the cash inventory a habit after any cash transaction. It only takes a moment, and keeping it current means you always have an accurate answer to "how much cash do I have right now?"
</Tip>
