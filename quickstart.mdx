---

After installing TransactApp, you are ready to start managing your finances. This guide walks you through the essential first steps: launching the app, recording an income or expense entry, checking your cash inventory, and viewing a report. The whole flow takes about five minutes.

<Note>
  If you have not installed TransactApp yet, see the [installation guide](/installation) before continuing.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch TransactApp">
    Open TransactApp from the desktop shortcut or the Start menu. When the app starts, ClickOnce first checks for updates. If a newer version is available, it downloads and applies the update before the main window appears — this is expected behavior and only takes a moment.

    The main window opens with the transaction list in the center, a navigation panel on the left, and a summary area at the top showing your current balance.

    <Tip>
      On first launch, the transaction list is empty and your balance shows zero. This is normal — TransactApp starts with a clean slate so you can enter your actual opening balance.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record your first transaction">
    To add an income or expense entry:

    1. Click the **New transaction** button in the toolbar.
    2. Select the transaction type: **Income** or **Expense**.
    3. Enter the amount.
    4. Choose or type a category (for example, "Salary" for income or "Groceries" for an expense).
    5. Set the date — it defaults to today.
    6. Optionally, add a note to describe the entry.
    7. Click **Save**.

    The transaction appears immediately in the list, and the balance summary at the top updates to reflect the new entry.

    <Tip>
      Start by recording your current account balance as an income entry with the category "Opening balance." This gives you an accurate starting point for tracking future transactions.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your cash inventory">
    If you handle physical cash and want to track how much you have on hand by denomination, navigate to the **Cash inventory** section from the left panel.

    Here you can:

    - Enter the quantity of each bill or coin denomination you currently have.
    - See the calculated total for each denomination and an overall cash total.
    - Update the counts as you spend or receive cash.

    <Note>
      The cash inventory is independent from your transaction log. It represents a snapshot of the physical cash you have at any given moment. Update it whenever you count your wallet or cash drawer.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your first report">
    Navigate to the **Reports** section from the left panel. Reports summarize your transaction data so you can see patterns in your income and spending.

    On your first visit:

    1. Select a date range (for example, the current month).
    2. Choose a report type — a summary by category or a full transaction list.
    3. Review the breakdown of income versus expenses for the selected period.

    <Tip>
      Even with just one or two transactions entered, running a report helps you confirm that your data was recorded correctly before you add a large amount of historical data.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep your data safe">
    TransactApp stores all data in a local SQLite database file on your machine. Since there is no cloud backup, it is a good habit to periodically copy the database file to an external drive or cloud storage folder.

    See [Data backup](/guides/data-backup) for the exact file location and recommended backup steps.
  </Step>
</Steps>
