Keeping your finances accurate and up to date takes more than just recording transactions — it requires a consistent routine. This guide walks you through how to use TransactApp effectively, from categorizing income and expenses to building a regular review habit that keeps your data meaningful over time.

## Core practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Categorize income and expenses consistently" defaultOpen={true}>
    Assigning a clear category to every transaction is the foundation of useful financial records. When categories are applied consistently, your reports become a reliable snapshot of where money comes from and where it goes.

    - Create categories that reflect your actual spending habits — for example, **Groceries**, **Utilities**, **Transport**, **Entertainment**, and **Income**.
    - Avoid catch-all categories like "Miscellaneous" for recurring entries. If you find yourself using it often, it's a sign that a new dedicated category is needed.
    - Use the same category name for the same type of expense every time. Inconsistent naming (e.g., "Food" vs. "Groceries") splits your data and makes reports harder to read.

    <Tip>
      Set up your categories before you start entering transactions. Deciding on a category structure upfront saves time and prevents inconsistencies later.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Enter transactions promptly and accurately">
    The most common source of errors in personal finance records is delayed or reconstructed entry. Entering a transaction from memory two weeks later increases the chance of mistakes.

    - Record each transaction the same day it happens, or at most within a day or two.
    - Use the date field to reflect the actual transaction date, not the entry date — this keeps your reports historically accurate.
    - Double-check amounts before saving. A misplaced decimal point (e.g., 12.50 entered as 125.0) is easy to overlook and hard to find later.

    <Tip>
      Keep TransactApp open in the background during the day so entering a transaction takes only a few seconds. The quicker the entry habit, the more accurate your records will be.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Track cash inventory alongside transactions">
    TransactApp includes a cash inventory tool that lets you record the physical cash you have on hand, separate from your transaction log. Using both together gives you a more complete view of your finances.

    - Update your cash inventory whenever you receive or spend cash, so it stays in sync with your transaction records.
    - Cross-reference your cash inventory balance with your transaction history periodically. A discrepancy can indicate a missed entry.
    - Use cash inventory entries to track cash you set aside for specific purposes, such as a spending envelope or a savings reserve.

    <Tip>
      After recording a cash transaction in the transactions view, update the cash inventory immediately — doing both steps together prevents the balance from drifting out of sync.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set up a weekly review habit">
    A brief weekly review helps you catch errors early, spot trends before they become problems, and stay aware of your financial position without waiting for a monthly summary.

    **Suggested weekly review checklist:**

    1. Open the reports view and scan the current week's transactions.
    2. Verify that every transaction has the correct category assigned.
    3. Check your cash inventory balance against the physical cash you have on hand.
    4. Look for any duplicate or missing entries.
    5. Note any unusual spikes in a category so you can investigate or account for them.

    <Tip>
      Pick a consistent day and time for your weekly review — for example, every Sunday evening. Attaching it to an existing habit makes it easier to maintain.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Conduct a monthly financial review">
    Once a month, take a broader look at your income and expenses to understand your overall financial health and identify areas to adjust.

    - Use the reports view to compare this month's spending against previous months in the same categories.
    - Verify that your recorded income matches what you actually received.
    - Reconcile your cash inventory with any cash you set aside or spent during the month.
    - Identify categories where spending is consistently higher than expected and consider whether a budget adjustment is needed.

    <Tip>
      Screenshot or note down your monthly totals before closing the month so you have a reference to compare against future periods.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
